A little about the woman behind every candle, and why she started Marigold & Co.

Hi, I'm Sandra

From kitchen experiments to a real business

It started on a cold November evening in 2021. My kitchen smelled like a spa gone wrong, and I had wax on my ceiling. But the candle I'd made — an amber and cedarwood blend I'd been tweaking for weeks — was perfect.

I'd been making candles as gifts for years, but that night something clicked. My friends kept asking where to buy them. My Etsy shop opened quietly the following January, and by March I'd sold out three times.

Three years later, Marigold & Co. is still me — still handpoured, still small-batch, still made at my studio table in Austin. Just with a lot less wax on the ceiling.

What I Believe In

The Marigold values

Every decision I make — from the wax I source to the boxes I ship in — comes back to these principles.

Natural, always

I use a soy-coconut wax blend with no paraffin, no synthetic dyes, and only phthalate-free fragrance oils. What you burn in your home should be clean.

Handmade, every time

No machines, no outsourcing. Every candle is poured, labelled, and quality-checked by me. It takes longer, but you can feel the difference.

Thoughtfully sustainable

Recycled card packaging, minimal plastic, and I encourage customers to reuse jars. Not perfect — but always trying to do better.

Small but personal

Being small means I can be personal. I reply to every message, I remember returning customers, and I love doing custom orders.

Behind the Scenes

How a Marigold candle is made

1. Blend & Test

Each scent goes through weeks of small-batch testing before it's ever listed.

2. Melt & Pour

Wax is melted at the right temperature, fragrance added precisely, then poured slowly.

3. Cure

Candles cure for at least 48 hours for a cleaner, stronger scent throw.

4. Pack & Ship

Packed in recycled kraft boxes, each candle ships within 2–3 business days.
